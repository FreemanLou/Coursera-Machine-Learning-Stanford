        Thanks, this is very helpful for clarifying my thoughts on Week 1 of the course.

        I have one comment about the last paragraph of your section "Gradient Descent - Multiple Variables Example" where you note that Ѳ1 and Ѳ2 should not be updated until you have computed the new value for both of them. Technically in the example you gave in that section I think it wouldn't matter, because the two variables are completely separate. The reason it matters in the MSE function is that the slope of Ѳ1 can be impacted by the value of Ѳ2 and vice versa, which isn't the case in your simpler example.

        In other words, in the J = Ѳ1*2 + Ѳ2*2 example I think you would get exactly the same series of values for Ѳ1 and Ѳ2 for a given learning rate, regardless of the update order. In the case of MSE you could get a different series of values because Ѳ1 is part of the derivative of Ѳ2 and vice versa.

        Disclaimers: Of course, it would still be a best practice to do simultaneous updates in any case. Also I am still an early stage learner myself so I could *definitely* be wrong about everything.
